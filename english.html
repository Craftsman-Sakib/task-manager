Present Simple Tense

The present simple tense is used to describe habits, general truths, and unchanging situations. Example: "I walk to school every day."

Past Continuous Tense

The past continuous tense is used to describe an ongoing action that happened at a specific point in the past. Example: "I was reading a book while he was playing games."

  • Formation :was /were+base form +ing.
  • I was watching TV all day yesterday .
  • It was raining yesterday evening.

Future Simple Tense

The future simple tense is used to express future actions. Use 'will' or 'be going to'. Example: I will visit my parents. It's going to rain.
